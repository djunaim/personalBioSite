About Me

Background: After getting my B.A. in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, I wanted to travel and see the world for what it is in its whole form, and not just a molecule. After a fun year and a half in Korea, I worked at Vanderbilt as a program coordinator for clinical research within the Cardiac Surgery Department. From there, I have been in clinical research ever since, whether that was in the hospital or the CRO level, and now, working at a health tech startup that is changing the way clinical trials are run.

Why development: As a Clinical Information Designer, I was indirectly introduced to HTML and some responsibilities of being a programmer. For example, performing acceptance testing during the project development period, functional testing projects to confirm search results are working correctly, and designing content in the application with the end user in mind. Being able to create a product from start to finish and seeing the end result sparked my interest in becoming a developer. Currently, I am enrolled in the Part-time, Full-Stack Web Development Program to further develop my career in web development and become a Software Engineer. Merging my 2 experiences of clinical trias and now tech, I want to bridge the gap between technology and clinical research.

Fun thing about development: Instant gratification. Being able to see what you've done to *hopefully* display on the page is really nice when everything goes according to plan.
